# About

## Our History

The Akuapem Traditional Council is one of the oldest and most respected traditional institutions in Ghana, with a history spanning several centuries. Rooted in the Guan and Akan heritage of the Akuapem Ridge, the Council has served as the custodian of cultural traditions, the arbiter of disputes, and the governing authority uniting 17 principal towns under the leadership of the Okuapehene. From its role in welcoming the Basel missionaries in the 19th century to its modern efforts in community development, the Council continues to bridge the past and the future for the people of Akuapem.

1. The Traditional Council

The traditional council is an ancient structure that goes back almost a millennia and initially was the body that governed a traditional state.

It is made up of a Supreme head in the form of a Omanhene (King) who is of royal lineage a Obaahema (Queenmother) various chiefs, elders and other prominent members of the state.

This body would consult and makes decisions on behalf of the state as well as act as custodians of land, which it holds in trust for all the people.

The role and significance of any traditional council to this day is very much relevant and quite influential.

Lower Axim Tradition Council consists of the following chiefs and persons, some of which hold dual or multiple roles.

All sub and divisional Chiefs, before being enstooled, have sworn an oath of allegiance to the Omanhene, the stool and traditional state.

All chiefs remain apolitical in their roles.

Lower Axim Traditional Council continues to serve the traditional state and its people and is making great efforts to push for development and improve the lives of the people of Axim state.
